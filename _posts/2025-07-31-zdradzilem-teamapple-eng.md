## Pro tip for iMessage

I didn‚Äôt think about this at all, but before inserting the SIM card into the new phone, you must deactivate iMessage. Especially if it‚Äôs an eSIM, not a physical card, because activating it on the new phone automatically disables the old one. The problem is, if you forget this like I did, iMessage stays active, and on the new Android phone you won‚Äôt receive SMS messages from people you previously messaged via iMessage. Their messages will simply disappear or go to other devices where you had iMessage configured. I realized this only about a week later when several friends told me, "Hey, I already told you about this!" However, for forgetful folks like me who switched their SIM hastily, there is a lifeline. Just go to the Deregister iMessage page, go to the "No longer have your iPhone?" section, select your country, enter your phone number, enter the CAPTCHA, and hit "Send Code". Shortly after, you‚Äôll receive a 6-digit code via SMS on your new phone, which you enter in the box below and confirm. If everything is done correctly, iMessage will be completely deactivated for your number, and all messages will come as regular SMS on your new Android phone.
